About this place

Welcome to the legendary Greenboim Estate — a one-of-a-kind property nestled somewhere between “guest room” and “just sit anywhere, there's room on the couch.” Your host, Hadar, is a seasoned Superhost who has been involuntarily hosting siblings, cousins, parents, and the occasional stray friend since August 2025 — and hasn’t had a break since. The vibe is cozy-chaotic: you'll be treated like family (because you are), which means you'll be offered snacks, then immediately judged for which ones you chose. The room comes fully furnished with Hadar's unsolicited opinions and a duvet so aggressively clean you'll be afraid to touch it. Minimum stay: however long it takes before the gentle hints about heading home begin.

🚿 The Downstairs Shower Experience™

The downstairs shower is, technically, a shower. It has walls. It has a showerhead. It has water — and that’s where the certainties end. Temperature operates on a proprietary spectrum best described as Scalding → Glacial → Briefly Human → Scalding Again, cycling through these states on an interval no guest has yet been able to predict. Hydrologists have been consulted. They declined to comment.

Water pressure is directly linked to household activity: if anyone upstairs flushes, breathes near a tap, or looks at the kitchen sink with intent, you will know about it instantly and physically. The curtain rod has a personality. The hook that held the towel fell off sometime in 2019 and has entered a state of semi-retirement on the floor. Guests are encouraged to bring a plan.

Past guests have described the experience as: “a humbling four minutes,” “genuinely clarifying,” and “fine, honestly, once you stop expecting it to make sense.” Average emotional arc: confusion → acceptance → mild triumph.

⚠️ Host note from Udi: “It’s totally fine. You just need to turn the handle past the resistance point, stand slightly to the left, ignore the sound it makes at startup, and definitely don’t touch the blue knob. I’ve been meaning to fix it.” (Note from Hadar: He has not been meaning to fix it.)
